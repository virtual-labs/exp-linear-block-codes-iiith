  • Linear Independence
  • Vector Space
Instructions
    Quick theory overview:
  • The set of vectors \(\mathbf{v_1, v_2, . . ., v_m} \in \mathbb{F}_{2^n} \) are said to be linearly dependent, if there exists a set of \(n\) scalars \(a_1, a_2, . . ., a_m \in \mathbb{F}_{2^n}\), not all zero, such that \( a_1 \mathbf{v_1} + a_2 \mathbf{v_2} + . . . + a_m\mathbf{v_m}= \mathbf{0} \).
  • The set of vectors \( \mathbf{v_1, v_2, \dots, v_m} \) are said to be linearly independent, if and only if the equation \( a_1 \mathbf{v_1} + a_2 \mathbf{v_2} + \dots + a_m \mathbf{v_m}= \mathbf{0} \) has \(a_1 = a_2 =\dots = a_m=0 \) as the only solution.

    Procedure:
  • Enter the values of \(a_1, a_2, \dots, a_m \in \{0, 1\}\) in the corresponding fields.
  • Choose one of the options from the drop-down and click on Submit.
  • The correctness of the entered answer is displayed in Observations.
